## Automotive Microcontrollers

###### Cortex-M0+/M4 and Cortex-M7-Based Cores
A 32-bit MCU scalable family for automotive body, zone control and electrification applications.

The S32M family offers scalability, high-performance for streamlined control of BLDC and PMSM
motors used for in-vehicle applications such as pumps, fans, sunroofs, seat positioning, seat
belt pretensioners, trunk openers and more.

## Wireless Connectivity Microcontrollers
#### [Bluetooth®\  Low Energy (BLE)](https://www.nxp.com/products/wireless-connectivity/bluetooth-low-energy:BLUETOOTH-LOW-ENERGY-BLE)
Our Bluetooth Low Energy (BLE) solutions provide complete SoCs, a host BLE stack and many GATT
profiles and services.
#### [Sub-GHz ISM\  Bands](https://www.nxp.com/products/wireless-connectivity/sub-ghz-ism-bands:SUB-1-GHZ-WIRELESS-SOLUTIONS)
These high-performance, bidirectional sub-GHz radios are capable of operating over a wide
frequency range including 315, 433, 470, 868, 915, 928 and 960 MHz in the license-free
industrial, scientific and medical (ISM) frequency bands.
#### [Thread](https://www.nxp.com/products/wireless-connectivity/thread:THREAD-PROTOCOL)
Thread is a simplified IPv6-based mesh networking protocol developed by technology companies for
connecting products around the home to each other, to the Internet and to the cloud.
#### [Zigbee](https://www.nxp.com/products/wireless-connectivity/zigbee:ZIGBEE-PROTOCOL)
Zigbee is a networking protocol defined by the Zigbee Alliance for low-cost, low-power, wireless
control and monitoring solutions.
## Legacy Microcontrollers
#### [MAC7100](https://www.nxp.com/products/processors-and-microcontrollers/legacy-mpu-mcus/mac7xxx-automotive-controllers:MAC7XXXAUTO)
The MAC7xxx family of MCUs is a pin-compatible family of 32-bit flash memory-based devices
developed specifically for embedded automotive applications.
#### [VFxxx Controller Solutions based on Arm Cores](https://www.nxp.com/products/processors-and-microcontrollers/legacy-mpu-mcus/vfxxx-controller:VFXXX)
VFxxx devices are built on an asymmetrical-multiprocessing architecture using Arm® cores as the
anchor for the platform.
